    AI YUSUP

    Artist Ai Yusup works in collaboration with nonprofit organizations focusing on building interfaith dialogue and advocating for social justice of minorities such as people of color, immigrants, women, and LGBTQ.

    DECOMPARTMENTALISM

    As a protest art movement, Decompartmentalism attempts to address the human state of having inconsistent and conflicting cognitions, beliefs, values, attitudes, and behaviors as a result of a highly fragmentalized society and politics.

    ABSTRACT ART

    Abstract Art is a tool that has been long used by the autor to communicate with people about her innermost being who always wants to be free and independent of societal norms and expectations.

  • ABOUT

    Theodor Adorno once famously wrote in his critiques, "all art is an uncommitted crime,"
    meaning that every artwork is created to challenge the status quo by its very nature.

    Same is true about Ai Yusup and her works, whose art is known to be political. Using
    her art to address oppression and marginalization of people of color, immigrants, women,
    and LGBTQ in the United States, Ai Yusup challenges sociopolitical issues, hierarchies and
    traditional expectations imposed upon vulnerable by those in power.

    Decompartmentalism as a protest art project led by the author has emerged into the
    individual form of politically engaging artistic expression. The philosophy of
    Decompartmentalism revolves around dismantling systems built as a tool of oppression,
    violence, injustice, and inequalities. It's a fair movement of protest against everything
    that's unfair.
